# Cover Letter Playbook: Knowledge

A cover letter exists to do one thing: connect **this company's need** to **the owner's
real evidence**, fast. ≤ 300 words, 3 paragraphs, specific, human. Match tone to the
company (see `tone_matrix.md`). Pull facts only from `_system/PROFILE.md` / `base_cv.md`.

## Structure
1. **Hook (2–3 sentences).** Open with something concrete about the company or role, a
   product, a value, the team's work, a sector angle from companyResearch, then state
   the role. *Never* "I am writing to apply for…" or "I am passionate about…".
2. **Proof (4–5 sentences).** Why the owner fits: the 2–3 strongest JD-matching skills,
   backed by real work from base_cv.md. Mirror JD language where honest. Address the
   role's core need head-on. Frame experience level honestly (an early-career owner is
   hungry + fast-learning, not a gap; a senior owner leads with depth).
3. **Close (2–3 sentences).** Forward-looking: what they'd contribute + interest in the
   mission; a confident, low-friction sign-off.

## Hooks that work
- Reference a specific product/initiative ("…your work on [product]…").
- Tie to the mission/sector the companyResearch surfaced.
- Lead with the strongest matching skill the JD emphasises.

## Rules
- One page, ≤ 300 words. No em dashes. No clichés. No invented experience.
- Name the company and role correctly (kills "spray and pray" perception).
- Sound like a sharp human engineer, not a template.
- Write in the JD's language; note other language abilities where PROFILE.md supports it.
- Reuse what works: when the owner approves a letter, store the winning
  structure/phrasing in `agentMemory.successfulTemplates` and reuse for similar roles.

## Output
Write `cover_letter.docx` (+ `.pdf` for mobile) into the job folder; upload both to
Drive and record the links. Everything waits for the owner's approval before being
sent anywhere.
