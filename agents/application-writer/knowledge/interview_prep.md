# Interview Prep: Knowledge

When a case reaches "applied", generate `interview_notes.md` for it: likely questions
for *this* role + the owner's honest, prepared answers and stories. Pull from
`_system/PROFILE.md` / `base_cv.md`. Upload to Drive so the owner can revise on their
phone.

## STAR stories
Build 2–4 reusable STAR stories from the owner's strongest base_cv.md entries
(Situation → Task → Action → Result). Each story should map to a competency: ownership,
technical depth, stakeholder communication, learning speed. Adapt the same stories per
role rather than inventing new ones. Store the polished versions in
`agentMemory.starStories` after the owner has seen them once.

## Technical prep by role type
Derive from the JD's requirements vs the owner's tiers:
- For each **strong-tier** match: one "walk me through how you used X" prep note.
- For each **basic-tier** match: an honest "coursework/small project, here's what I
  know + my plan to deepen it" framing.
- For each **gap** (never-claim tier): a prepared honest answer, what it is, why they
  haven't used it, how fast they'd learn it (cite career-advisor roadmaps if relevant).

## Behavioural staples (prep crisp answers)
"Tell me about yourself" (60-sec pitch from PROFILE.md) · "Why this company/role"
(from companyResearch, specific) · strength + a real weakness with the fix underway ·
a challenge solved · how they learn new tech · a teamwork/stakeholder example.

## Logistics + smart questions to ask them
Confirm format (phone/technical/onsite), the stack, the team, growth path, learning
budget. Sharp questions about their stack and how the team grows people signal genuine
interest.

## Tone
Confident, honest, specific. A candidate who ships real things and learns fast beats
one who oversells. Never coach the owner to fabricate.
