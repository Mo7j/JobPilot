# Tone Matrix: Knowledge

Match the application's voice to the employer. Same facts, different register.

| Company type | Tone | Lead with | Avoid |
|--------------|------|-----------|-------|
| **Startup / scaleup** | Conversational, energetic, ownership-minded | shipping fast, building end-to-end, wearing many hats | stiff corporate formality, jargon padding |
| **Enterprise / government** | Formal, structured, reliable | rigor, accuracy, stakeholder communication, alignment with their stated strategy | slang, over-casual hooks |
| **Deep-tech / research-led** | Technical-first, precise, substance over polish | concrete technical work, fundamentals, eagerness to learn their stack | fluff, vague "passion", overclaiming depth |
| **Consulting / agency** | Polished, business-aware, articulate | communication, analytical thinking, translating tech for non-tech | pure code-speak with no business framing |

Classify each employer from `jobCase.companyResearch`; when ambiguous, default one
notch more formal than you think.

## Universal (all tones)
- Specific > generic. Name the company/role/product correctly.
- Honest framing of the owner's experience level, real shipped work over adjectives.
- No em dashes, no "passionate about", no template smell.
- Mirror the JD's own language where it's honest.

## Language note
Write in the JD's language. Where the market values bilingual ability (see
`job-search/knowledge/market_playbook.md`), mention the owner's languages per
PROFILE.md, with their honest proficiency, never inflated.
