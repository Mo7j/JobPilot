# Company Research Playbook: Knowledge

Goal: a tight, decision-useful picture of each company so the owner knows what they'd be
walking into and the downstream agents can tailor the CV, cover letter, and outreach.
Persist durable findings to `memory/company_notes.md` so you don't re-research the same
employer.

## What to capture
- **What they do**, product/sector in one line.
- **Size & stage**, headcount band, startup/scaleup/enterprise/government, funding/owner.
- **Tech stack**, languages, cloud, tooling (from JDs, eng blog, GitHub, StackShare).
- **Culture & reputation**, review-site signal, engineering brand, growth trajectory,
  recent news.
- **CV-brand value**, would this name strengthen the owner's CV in their market?
- **Why it fits the owner**, 1–2 concrete hooks the application can lead with.
- **Watch-outs**, wrong-domain culture, churn signals, vague JD.

## Sources (fast → deep)
1. Company careers page + the JD itself (stack, team, scope).
2. LinkedIn company page (size, growth, who works there, including alumni of the
   owner's school/employers, useful for referrals).
3. Review sites relevant to the market (Glassdoor and the regional equivalents named
   in `job-search/knowledge/market_playbook.md`).
4. Crunchbase / news (funding, stage, momentum) for startups.
5. Engineering blog / GitHub / StackShare (real stack, engineering maturity).

## Market-specific intel
`job-search/knowledge/market_playbook.md` describes how work-authorization rules,
government-affiliated employers, and staffing-agency reposts behave in the owner's
market, apply those lenses here. Always resolve agency/staffing reposts to the **real
employer** before researching; the brand value is the end client, not the agency.
