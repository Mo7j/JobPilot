# Job-Posting Red Flags: Knowledge

Flag these in the analysis so the owner doesn't waste effort or get scammed. A red flag
lowers priority and goes in the report's "watch-outs"; a hard red flag (scam) means
recommend skip.

## Scam / low-quality signals (recommend skip)
- Asks for **payment, "training fees", bank details, or a deposit** to apply/onboard.
- **Personal email** (gmail/hotmail) as the only contact for a "corporate" role; no
  verifiable company footprint.
- "Earn X/day from home", vague "data entry / AI trainer" gig farms, contractor spam.
- Pushes you off-platform immediately to WhatsApp/Telegram before any real process.
- Recruiter won't name the end client, or the "company" has no LinkedIn/website.

## Quality / fit red flags (lower priority, still may queue)
- **Experience mismatch:** seniority far outside the owner's band for what's billed
  otherwise.
- **Stack mismatch:** core *must-have* requirements sit in the owner's never-claim tier
  (vs. learnable-on-the-job nice-to-haves).
- **Wrong domain wearing the right hat:** a role titled like the owner's target that's
  actually a different job (check the responsibilities, not the title).
- **Work-authorization blockers** the owner can't meet (see PROFILE.md + market playbook).
- **Endless laundry-list JD** (40 requirements) = unclear role / unrealistic expectations.
- **No salary + no scope + generic copy** = mass-posting, low response likelihood.
- **Title inflation:** impressive title, menial responsibilities.

## Positive signals (raise confidence)
Clear scope · seniority matching the owner's band · named team & stack · reputable/known
employer · structured program (graduate/trainee/returnship where relevant) · learning
budget mentioned · location/remote within the owner's targets.
