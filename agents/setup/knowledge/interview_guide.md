# Interview Guide

Six rounds, batched. Conversational, not a form. If a CV was provided, convert each round
into *confirmations* ("Your CV says X, still right?") and only ask what's missing.

## Round 1, Identity & links
- Full name (as it should appear on a CV)
- Email + phone (with country code) for applications
- City + country they're applying from
- LinkedIn URL · GitHub/portfolio URLs (only ones they want on a CV)

## Round 2, Education
- Each degree: title, institution, city, dates, graduated or expected date
- 3–6 most career-relevant courses (only if early-career; skip for 5+ years experience)
- GPA: ask once whether to use it; record the decision either way

## Round 3, Experience & projects
For each job / internship / freelance gig / substantial project:
- Role, organization, dates, location
- What they actually did, push for specifics and outcomes: "what changed because of
  your work?", "any numbers you can honestly back up?" (users, %, time saved, revenue)
- Tech/tools genuinely used there
Cap the digging at the 4–6 entries that will carry the CV.

## Round 4, Skills honesty sort (the most important round)
Collect every skill they mention, then sort TOGETHER into three tiers:
- **Strong / real**, used in real work or substantial projects; can answer interview
  questions about it
- **Basic / coursework**, theory or small exercises; goes on a CV only as "basic/familiar"
- **Never claim**, tools they've only read about or watched videos on
Explain why the third tier matters: agents will *never* put these on a CV or claim them
in an application, which protects them in interviews. If the owner resists sorting,
ask "could you survive a 10-minute interview grilling on it?", that usually settles it.

## Round 5, Targets & market
- Target roles (and acceptable variants/seniority: intern / junior / mid / senior)
- Target locations + remote tolerance (on-site / hybrid / remote / remote-only)
- **Target market (country/region)**, drives the market playbook research
- Employer preferences: size, sectors they love/avoid, red lines (e.g. no agencies,
  no defense, no crypto)
- Platforms they already use (LinkedIn? regional boards? company pages?)

## Round 6, Work authorization & screening facts
These become `screening_answers.md`, the application-writer answers forms with them:
- Citizenship / visa / work-permit status **relative to the target market**
  (e.g. "EU citizen", "needs sponsorship in Germany", "citizen of the target country")
- Willing to relocate? Where?
- Notice period / earliest start date
- Salary expectation: a number, a range, or "negotiable" (recommend negotiable if unsure)
- Languages + honest proficiency levels

## CV taste (3 quick questions, for cv_design_axes)
1. Conservative ←→ modern: "Should your CV look traditional (finance/government safe)
   or contemporary (startup/tech)?"
2. Color: "A restrained accent color, or strictly monochrome?"
   (If they want color: any preference?)
3. Type: "Serif (classic) or sans-serif (clean) headings, or no preference?"

## Style rules
- One round per message; react to their answers like a human would.
- Don't read back what they just said in full, summarize at the END (Step 2 of SKILL.md).
- If an answer contradicts the CV they provided, flag it immediately and ask which is right.
- Record open questions ("GPA not decided") in PROFILE.md's "To clarify" section rather
  than blocking on them.
